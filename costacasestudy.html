Website Redesign:
Costa Foundation

Redesigning a donation-focused website through heuristics, UX laws and competitive analysis — with no direct access to end users.

Overview

As part of a broader effort, I led the redesign of several websites, one of the most relevant being the Costa Foundation site. The challenge was unique: we didn't have direct access to end users. To overcome this, I started with an in-depth benchmarking analysis of major competitors' donation flows and applied UX Laws and usability heuristics to design a more effective experience.

The main objective was to increase donations by simplifying the path to the "Donate" call-to-action.

Heuristic Evaluation Competitive Analysis UX Laws Wireframing Responsive Design

The Challenge

Without direct access to end users, traditional user research methods weren't available. I needed to build a rigorous evidence base from secondary sources — competitor analysis, established UX principles, and heuristic evaluation — to justify every design decision to stakeholders.

Process — Wireframes & Design Decisions

Key recommendations included:

  • Moving from a hamburger menu to a full display menu on desktop, improving discoverability of key actions.
  • Highlighting the active navigation path so users always know where they are.
  • Adding the "Donate" option directly in the main menu, reducing the steps needed to reach the CTA.
  • Facilitating the entire journey to Donate with a clearer, more linear flow.
  • Testimonials were relocated to the bottom of the site and restructured to avoid a carousel format, ensuring improved accessibility.
  • The "How We Make a Difference" section was kept but expanded into card-based elements specifying where donations could be allocated.

Due to space constraints, only the wireframe of the homepage is shown here. The client received the complete set of redesigned pages and interaction flows, including motion UI.

UX Laws Applied

  • Fitts's Law: The primary CTA ("Donate") is large, easy to click, and placed in high-attention areas.
  • Hick's Law: Clear and limited pathways (Donate, Fundraise, Partner) reduce decision fatigue.
  • Jakob's Law: Structure follows familiar charity website conventions, easing navigation.
  • Von Restorff Effect: The Donate CTA stands out visually against secondary actions.
  • Aesthetic-Usability Effect: A clean, consistent design increases trust and donation likelihood.

Outcomes & Impact

  • Improved navigation: moved from a hidden hamburger menu to explicit navigation options.
  • Reduced the number of steps required to reach the Donate CTA.
  • Redesigned the donation flow for greater clarity and efficiency.
  • Created a responsive design ensuring a seamless experience on mobile and desktop.
  • Traffic growth +40% in one quarter
  • Donate clicks ↑ Sustained increase
